Visualizing California's Largest Wildfire in History

August Complex (2020)

The August Complex Fire was the largest wildfire in Californian history, covering over 1 million acres, an area larger than the state of Rhode Island. Approximately 77,000 people evacuated to surrounding counties, which we will visualize here. For this visualization, we utilized US mobility data along with the California wildfire geolocation data. The 7 larger nodes marked in purple are the counties (Mendocino, Humbolt, Trinity, Tehama, Glenn, Lake, and Colusa) that were directly affected by the August complex fire, according to CA fire. In the graph, we show where people have migrated to from one county to another during the fire.
